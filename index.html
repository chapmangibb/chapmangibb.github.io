Psychotherapy

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy is the therapy of choice within the NHS, and clinical studies have demonstrated that the efficacy of CBT is enhanced when used in conjunction with hypnosis. However, I offer Cognitive Behavioural Therapy as a stand alone treatment, when required.

Cognitive Behavioural Hypnotherapy is an integrative and technically eclectic Psychotherapy. It combines classical hypnotherapy with the techniques and concepts from Cognitive Behavioural Therapy.

Hypnotherapy

Hypnosis is a naturally occurring 'state' that combines relaxation and focused awareness. Our brains 'slip' into hypnosis, commonly referred to as 'trance' in our everyday lives - we just don't realize it! For example, daydreaming, falling asleep, or reading.

Hypnotherapy combines proven therapeutic approaches with the benefits of hypnosis, put simply people are not treated with hypnosis but in hypnosis. It utilizes the natural trance state, by accessing the 'unconscious' part of the brain, where positive suggestions are employed to effect desired changes in behaviour, be it physical, emotional or psychological.

Hypnotherapy is an active process, where you are not asleep, you remain IN CONTROL AT ALL TIMES, and cannot be made to act, think or feel anything that is not experienced as appropriate for you.

Sessions

In the first session, every individual has their needs assessed and is then treated accordingly, utilizing the most up to-date therapeutic tools appropriate to the presenting problem.

Typically, after 3 1hr sessions, individuals experience improvement, from that point a collaborative decision is taken about further treatment required. It is an ethical aim of CBH practitioners to keep the number of treatment sessions to a minimum.
